What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, gaming forums, or email to intimidate, harass, or humiliate others. It often involves spreading rumors, sending threatening messages, or sharing harmful content aimed at causing emotional distress.

Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying invades personal spaces, making it hard for victims to escape, as it follows them into their homes and private lives through their devices.

The Consequences of Cyberbullying

  • Low self-esteem and feelings of isolation
  • Anxiety, depression, and suicidal thoughts
  • Decreased academic or professional performance
  • Strained relationships with family and friends

The psychological toll of cyberbullying makes it crucial to address and prevent such behavior.

How to Prevent Cyberbullying

Think Before You Post

Consider the impact of your words and actions before sharing them online.

Educate Yourself

Learn about the signs and consequences of cyberbullying to recognize and address it effectively.

Promote Positivity

Use social platforms to encourage kindness, respect, and inclusion.
